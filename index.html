The questions that matter

Why did I leave Explorin Academy? ‚ñº

Two fundamental reasons drove my decision to move on:

Reason 01

Lack of constant kick due to poor talent density. The market I was operating in didn't provide the intellectual stimulation and high-calibre talent interactions that fuel my best work. I need to be surrounded by people who push me ‚Äî and that environment had plateaued.

Reason 02

Lack of vision alignment with my partner. We were unable to align on a common pivot for the company's direction. When co-founders can't agree on where the ship should head, the ship doesn't move. It was time to acknowledge that and chart a new course.

What kind of opportunity am I seeking now? ‚ñº

In the next opportunity, I am looking for five essential ingredients:

1. Wealth ‚Äî Money + Equity/Carry
I took a huge risk bootstrapping a startup right after college, and it didn't translate into financial security. I'm getting married this quarter ‚Äî last week of April ‚Äî and I need to be in a position where money is no longer a constraint on my decisions. Beyond salary, I want to align my financial interest with long-term commitment through equity or carry in investments. When my skin is in the game, I show up differently.
2. Mentor + People

Successful ‚Äî I want to learn the art of making businesses from someone who has done it multiple times. Theory doesn't cut it. I need proximity to a practitioner.

Value Alignment ‚Äî I'm a strong believer that when values are aligned with the people you work with, synergies emerge and miracles become possible.

Mentorship ‚Äî I am going to be a product of the person I work for or work alongside. The quality of mentor I am fortunate enough to find will directly shape the mentor I become for others.

3. Canvas ‚Äî Degree of Exposure
The value I add while unleashing my hidden talent depends entirely on the width, breadth, and depth of exposure across all dimensions of a business ‚Äî customers, team, product, mission, vision, values, the business idea, the speed. I need an environment that's as expansive as a globe ‚Äî global or at least country-level exposure ‚Äî so I can learn how things work from a macroeconomic vantage point. The bigger the canvas, the bigger the art.
4. Dream or Portfolio of Dreams

The ultimate driving force that ignites my hidden powers is the kind of dreams I surrender myself towards. I've figured there are two paths:

The Builder's Zone ‚Äî Become a Chief of Staff or close to the CEO/Founder, helping with everything needed to accelerate the ship and achieve high, sustainable acceleration.

The Investor's Path ‚Äî Hold a portfolio of dreams through pre-seed, seed, VC, growth-stage, or PE vehicles. Investing in multiple dreamers simultaneously ‚Äî multiplying impact across the ecosystem.

5. Staying Relevant
What I care about most is never again experiencing the FOMO I felt in my previous business. As long as I remain resourceful and relevant in accordance with rapidly changing trends ‚Äî economics, currencies, how businesses work, how people interact, communicate, and travel ‚Äî I can bring forth the most out of me. I need to stay relevant so I earn attention and make impact aligned with where the world is headed.
Where might I need handholding? ‚ñº
Core of Tech

I have only built and run a service-based business. I genuinely want to learn how tech-heavy products are architected, scaled, and optimised at the company level.

Example: If the task is to figure out how to optimise cloud costs, I would need the tech team to walk me through the fundamentals ‚Äî where cloud is being used, general practices, cost drivers, and trade-offs. I'd bring the strategic thinking and execution energy, but I'd need to be taught the technical substrate first.

I'm not afraid of this gap ‚Äî I'm excited by it. My track record shows I learn fast when the environment is right. I just want to be upfront about where the learning curve sits.

What are my key learnings? ‚ñº
Learning 01

Exponential learning happens when you are in a room with people chasing bolder visions and smarter than you. The ceiling of my growth at Explorin was directly set by the calibre of people around me. The moment I outgrew the room, growth flatlined. I will never again settle for an environment that doesn't stretch me.

Learning 02

Investing in the future. While building a service-based business for 5.5 years, I was heads-down in execution ‚Äî away from the rapid shifts happening in technology and AI. When I stepped out of Explorin and looked up, I was struck by how fast the industry had moved. It was both humbling and electrifying. I realized the world needs bold people who can leverage these shifts to create economic and social impact at scale ‚Äî to make human lives genuinely easier. The most exciting place to operate is at the intersection of what's happening now and what will be happening a decade from now. That's where I want to be.
